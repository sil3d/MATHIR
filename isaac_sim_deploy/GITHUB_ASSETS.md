### 🚀 MATHIR: The End of "Driver Amnesia"

**MATHIR** (**M**emory-**A**ugmented **T**ensor **H**ybrid with **I**ntelligent **R**outing) is a breakthrough neural architecture designed to solve a critical flaw in autonomous driving: **Catastrophic Forgetting**.

Unlike traditional RNNs (LSTMs/GRUs) which struggle to retain context over long horizons, MATHIR introduces a biological-inspired **Hierarchical Memory System** capable of recalling critical road events (gradients, friction, obstacles) thousands of steps later.

#### ⚡ Key Innovations
- **🧠 Triple Hierarchical Memory**: Distinct Working, Episodic, and Semantic memory modules (vs single cell in LSTM).
- **📐 Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC)**:  Stabilizes gradients on a Riemannian manifold to prevent explosion during long-term backpropagation.
- **🌊 Sinkhorn Projection**: Uses optimal transport theory to decide *exactly* what to forget and what to keep.
- **🧬 Differentiable Plasticity**: Weights adapt in real-time to sudden environmental shifts (rain, snow, sensor failure).

#### 🏆 "Torture Test" Benchmark Results
MATHIR was pitted against an optimized LSTM in a hostile, noise-injected environment ("Torture Spec").
